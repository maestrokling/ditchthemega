Best Alternatives to Amazon for Electronics

Electronics is one of the easiest categories to move away from Amazon. Several major retailers match prices, have physical stores for returns and support, and don't compete with their own marketplace sellers the way Amazon does.

Best Buy

Best for: General consumer electronics

Price vs Amazon: Price-match guarantee — they match Amazon prices

Shipping: Free shipping on most orders; same-day pickup at stores nationwide

  • Price-match guarantee (show them the Amazon price, they match it)
  • Physical stores for hands-on demos and returns
  • Geek Squad for installation and support
  • Same-day in-store pickup
  • Extended warranty options

Tradeoffs:

  • Less selection than Amazon for niche components
  • Some items are Best Buy exclusives at higher prices
Verdict: Best default Amazon replacement for consumer electronics. Same price, better support, physical locations.

B&H Photo

Best for: Cameras, audio, professional equipment

Price vs Amazon: Competitive; often lower on professional gear

Shipping: Free shipping on most orders over $49

  • Exceptional selection of professional cameras, audio, video, and tech
  • Knowledgeable staff (famous for their expertise)
  • No marketplace sellers — everything sold by B&H directly
  • Trusted for high-value purchases

Tradeoffs:

  • Closed Friday nights and Saturdays (observes Shabbat)
  • Less strong for commodity electronics
Verdict: The best place to buy cameras, audio equipment, and professional tech. Often better than Amazon on price and far better on trust.

Newegg

Best for: PC components, peripherals, computer hardware

Price vs Amazon: Competitive to lower, especially for components

Shipping: Free shipping on many items; fast

  • Dominant selection for PC components (CPUs, GPUs, RAM, storage)
  • Detailed technical specs and compatibility tools
  • Strong community reviews from technical users

Tradeoffs:

  • Has expanded to third-party sellers; some quality issues
  • Less strong for general consumer electronics
Verdict: Best for PC builders and anyone buying computer components. Still the go-to for hardware.

Direct from manufacturer

Best for: Apple, Samsung, Sony, Dell, HP, and other major brands

Price vs Amazon: Often same or lower; warranty direct from manufacturer

Shipping: Free shipping; often includes setup support

  • Buy directly from Apple, Samsung, Sony, Dell, etc.
  • Manufacturer warranty and support with no middleman
  • Sometimes better deals (trade-in programs, bundles)
  • No counterfeit risk (a real problem on Amazon for certain electronics)

Tradeoffs:

  • Only covers that brand's products
Verdict: For major brand electronics (phone, laptop, TV), always check the manufacturer's website first. Amazon is a middleman here.

Micro Center

Best for: In-person electronics, especially PC components

Price vs Amazon: Often lower on components; famous for deals

Shipping: In-store only (25 US locations)

  • Legendary prices on PC components
  • Hands-on shopping experience
  • Knowledgeable staff

Tradeoffs:

  • Only 25 US locations; not available everywhere
  • Online selection limited
Verdict: If you have one near you, Micro Center beats Amazon on PC components consistently. Worth the trip.

Honest assessment

Amazon's electronics marketplace has a real counterfeit problem. Fake cables, fake batteries, fake memory cards sold by third-party sellers under misleading listings. Best Buy, B&H, Newegg, and manufacturer direct don't have this problem because they control their inventory. For anything where authenticity matters — camera equipment, cables, batteries — Amazon is actually the riskier option.

Bottom line

Best Buy for general electronics (price-match eliminates the cost advantage). B&H for professional gear. Newegg for PC components. Manufacturer direct for major brand purchases. Amazon is the middleman here — not the only option.
