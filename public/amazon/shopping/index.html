Amazon Shopping

The store that isn't just a store

Moderate migration

What it is

Amazon's core marketplace — where third-party sellers and Amazon's own brands compete on the same page, often with Amazon's private label listed first. Over 60% of product searches now start on Amazon. It's become default infrastructure for online shopping.

Honest assessment

For common items, alternatives exist and are often price-competitive or cheaper. For truly obscure, niche, or specialty items, Amazon may still be your only practical option. The goal isn't zero Amazon purchases forever — it's reducing dependency and conscious use rather than reflexive default.

What you lose

  • Unmatched selection breadth (billions of products)
  • Easy A-to-Z Guarantee returns
  • One-click reorder
  • The ability to find truly obscure items in one place

Data to export first

  • Order history report (Account → Order History → Download Order Reports)
  • Saved addresses (note them manually)
  • Payment methods (remove after canceling)

Alternatives by category

Books

  • Bookshop.org — Independent bookstore network. Same prices as Amazon. Profits go to local bookstores.
  • ThriftBooks — Used books at significant discount. Often cheaper than Amazon used.
  • Your local library (Libby) — Free. Ebooks and audiobooks. Criminally underused.

Electronics

  • Best Buy — Price-match guarantee. Same-day pickup. Geek Squad support. Often same price.
  • B&H Photo — Professional-grade electronics, cameras, audio. Exceptional for tech.
  • Newegg — PC components, peripherals. Competitive pricing.

Household & Cleaning

  • Grove Collaborative — Natural/sustainable household products. Subscribe-and-save model.
  • Target — Strong household category. Same-day pickup or delivery.
  • Brand-direct — Many brands (Method, Seventh Generation, Burt's Bees) sell direct with better pricing than Amazon.

Clothing & Apparel

  • Brand-direct websites — Nike, Patagonia, Levi's, etc. all sell direct, often with better selection and returns.
  • Poshmark / ThredUp — Secondhand. Often dramatically cheaper. Better for the planet.
  • Nordstrom — Free shipping and returns. Strong selection across price points.

Pet Supplies

  • Chewy — Often cheaper than Amazon for pet food. Auto-ship available. Exceptional customer service.

Groceries

  • Instacart — Works with your existing local grocery stores.
  • Walmart Grocery — Competitive pricing, strong selection.
  • Local grocery delivery — Many regional chains offer direct delivery without the Amazon middleman.

Handmade & Unique Items

  • Etsy — Handmade, vintage, craft. Supports independent makers directly.

Migration steps

  1. For your most frequent purchases, identify the brand. Check if they sell direct.
  2. Bookmark 2–3 category-specific retailers for your biggest spend categories.
  3. Install the Honey or Capital One Shopping extension — they surface better prices across retailers.
  4. Try Bookshop.org for your next book purchase. The price is the same.
  5. For recurring household items, set up Chewy (pets) or Grove (household) subscriptions.
