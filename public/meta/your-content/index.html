Your Digital Content

Your posts and photos are exportable. Your social graph is Meta's.

Meta doesn't sell you ebooks, movies, or music. Meta's product is you.

Your "content" on Meta is your posts, photos, messages, friendships, group memberships, memories, and — if you've been on Facebook since college — sixteen years of your life. Almost all of it is exportable. The technical lock-in is minimal. The emotional and social lock-in is enormous.

Section 1 — Content you own and can export

Facebook data

facebook.com/dyi (Download Your Information). Choose all time, JSON format, high quality media.

What's included: every post, photo, video, comment, every Messenger message including group chats, your friends list, every event, marketplace activity, pages liked, groups, check-ins, every ad you've clicked, and — often startlingly — every advertiser who uploaded your contact information to target you.

Allow days for large archives. Request before you're ready to delete, not the same day.

Instagram data

Instagram app → Settings → Your Activity → Download Your Information. Includes every photo/video you posted, Reels, DMs, followers/following list, comments, and likes.

WhatsApp data

Settings → Chats → Export Chat. One conversation at a time — there is no bulk export. This is a deliberate design choice. You get text files with attached media (photos, videos, voice messages) in standard formats.

Messenger conversations with people who have died

If you have Messenger conversations with someone who is no longer alive, export them before deleting your account. Once your account is deleted, these conversations are gone. This content may have deep personal value and exists nowhere else.

Section 2 — What you can't take with you

Your social graph

You can export your friends list as names and profile URLs. You cannot import a social graph into another platform. Your connections exist on Facebook because your connections are on Facebook.

Before deactivating: collect phone numbers and email addresses of the 30–50 people you actually want to stay in touch with. Not your 847 Facebook friends. The real ones. Get their numbers. Text them.

Facebook Groups

When you leave Facebook, you lose access to groups. For important communities (neighborhood, hobby, support groups), check whether they exist elsewhere — Discord, Reddit, a mailing list. Some people deactivate their profile but keep Messenger and group access. Facebook allows this.

Meta Quest VR purchases

Games purchased through the Meta Quest Store are tied to your Meta account. Delete your account and they're gone, no refund.

  • Keep your Meta account solely for VR. You can delete Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp while maintaining a Meta account for Quest. VR purchases persist.
  • Move to SteamVR for future PC VR purchases — more stable platform, longer track record.

Section 3 — The bigger picture

Meta's lock-in is social, not technical

Apple locks you in with hardware. Amazon with content. Google with data. Meta locks you in with people.

Your family is on Facebook. Your friends are on Instagram. Your group chat is on WhatsApp. The cost of leaving Meta is not lost content or lost purchases. It's social friction: the conversations you miss, the events you don't hear about, the slow drift from people you used to be connected to effortlessly.

The 30-day grace period on account deletion means you can find out what you'd actually lose before the decision is permanent. That's worth using.

Deleting your account correctly

Removing all your content and changing your name is not deletion. Your advertising profile still exists. Your data remains. Use the real deletion: facebook.com/help/delete_account. 30-day grace period. Deletion completes in up to 90 days. Some data (messages you sent to others) may persist in the other person's account.

The approach we don't recommend

Data scraping tools and fake deletion

Tools exist that can bulk-download your Instagram posts, your Facebook data, and in some cases the posts of others through browser automation or API scraping. Meta's Terms of Service prohibit automated scraping. Using these tools can result in account suspension or permanent ban. In some cases, Meta has pursued legal action against scraping tool operators.

For your own data, the Download Your Information tool is the legal path and provides the same or better output. We recommend using it.

Fake account deletion warning: Removing all your content and changing your name is not deletion. Your advertising profile remains. Your data stays on Meta's servers. Use the real deletion process: facebook.com/help/delete_account. The 30-day grace period exists — use it. After 30 days, deletion begins and is permanent.

Our position: The official export tools are comprehensive. The reason to use the tools over scrapers is that they're more complete, less risky, and you don't leave a trace of your own data behind on an unauthorized tool's servers.

What Meta genuinely does that nothing else does

  • Your social graph — the specific network of people you're connected to, the shared history, the groups you've been part of for years. This doesn't exist elsewhere and can't be transferred.
  • Memories / On This Day — the curated history of what you posted and who you were with, surfaced unexpectedly. This is by design. It keeps you on the platform. It's also, sometimes, genuinely meaningful.
  • Community groups — neighborhood groups, parent groups, hobby communities, support communities. Many have no equivalent elsewhere. Some do (Discord, Reddit, Nextdoor). Many don't.
  • Event discovery — for local events, Facebook Events has no real equivalent for the "what's happening near me this weekend" use case.
  • Messenger with the deceased — if you have message threads with people who are no longer alive, those conversations may exist nowhere else. Export them before leaving. This is one of Meta's genuinely irreplaceable things.
