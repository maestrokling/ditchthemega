YouTube

The hardest Google service to leave — honestly

⚠️ The privacy case

YouTube's recommendation algorithm is designed to maximize watch time, which often means maximizing emotional engagement. YouTube knows more about your interests, opinions, and information diet than almost any other platform. It also tracks your viewing even when you're not logged in via cookies and fingerprinting.

What it is

YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine and the dominant video platform with 2.7 billion monthly users. It's owned by Google and powers an enormous advertising surveillance apparatus. Your watch history, search history, and behavior on YouTube build one of the most detailed interest profiles Google maintains.

What you lose

  • The world's largest video library (genuinely irreplaceable for many use cases)
  • Creator subscriptions and notification system
  • YouTube Music (if you use it)
  • Live streaming content
  • YouTube Premium (ad-free + background play + YouTube Music)

Honest assessment

This is the hardest Google service to leave because the content itself is on YouTube. Unlike Gmail (you can move your email) or Google Search (you can use DuckDuckGo), YouTube videos are not available elsewhere. The strategy here is less "leave YouTube" and more "use YouTube on your terms, with less surveillance." Full departure is only realistic if you genuinely don't need it.

The privacy minimum

If you can't leave YouTube entirely, these reduce the surveillance without giving up content: use Invidious or a privacy-respecting browser extension (uBlock Origin + SponsorBlock), watch logged out, or use YouTube via a VPN. YouTube Premium ($13.99/month) removes ads but doesn't reduce Google's data collection.

Alternatives

  • Invidious (YouTube without Google) — Open-source YouTube front-end. Watches YouTube videos without Google knowing. No tracking, no algorithm, no ads. Find an instance at invidious.io. Best option for people who need YouTube content but want privacy.
  • Odysee / LBRY — Decentralized video platform. Many YouTubers cross-post here. No algorithmic recommendation rabbit holes. Smaller catalog but growing.
  • Nebula $5/month or $30/year — Creator-owned streaming platform. Many popular educational YouTubers (CGP Grey, Wendover, Kurzgesagt, etc.) post here early or exclusively. Ad-free. Subscription supports creators directly.
  • Vimeo — Better video quality, filmmaker-focused. Niche but excellent for arts/documentary content.
  • PeerTube — Decentralized, open-source video hosting. Federated network of instances. Very small catalog currently. For the technically inclined.

Migration steps

  1. Export your subscription list and watch history via Google Takeout
  2. Try Invidious for 2 weeks — it plays YouTube videos without the tracking
  3. Check which of your subscribed creators are on Nebula (many educational channels are)
  4. If you use YouTube Music, see the music alternatives page for replacements
  5. For creators you support financially, consider direct Patreon support instead of YouTube channel memberships
