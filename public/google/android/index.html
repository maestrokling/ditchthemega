Android & Google Mobile

The phone in your pocket — and what it reports back

⚠️ The privacy case

Research has shown that a stock Android device with default settings sends location data to Google roughly every 20 minutes, even with location services "off." Google Play Services runs persistently in the background with elevated permissions. Your Android phone is, by design, a Google data collection device that also makes calls.

What it is

Android is Google's mobile operating system, running on roughly 72% of the world's smartphones. A standard Android device with Google services sends data to Google servers constantly — location, app usage, contacts, call logs, and more. Google Services Framework, Google Play Services, and the suite of Google apps are deeply embedded in most Android phones.

What you lose

  • The Google Play Store (largest app ecosystem)
  • Google Pay
  • Google Assistant integration
  • Seamless Google account integration

Honest assessment

This is the most complex migration on this page. Leaving Android fully means either switching to iPhone or running a de-Googled Android (like GrapheneOS on a Pixel). Both are significant commitments. The more realistic middle ground: use iPhone, or use Android with privacy-respecting app choices while minimizing Google service use. Full de-Googled Android is for the technically committed only.

The privacy minimum

If you're staying on Android and not ready for a full switch: Remove or disable all Google apps you don't use. Use DuckDuckGo instead of Chrome. Turn off all Google location history in account settings. Use Signal instead of Google Messages. Use a VPN. These don't eliminate Google data collection but reduce it.

Alternatives

  • iPhone (iOS) — Apple's privacy practices are significantly better than Google's by design — Apple's business model doesn't depend on data collection. App Tracking Transparency requires apps to ask permission before tracking. iMessage and FaceTime are end-to-end encrypted. The most practical full Android alternative for most people.
  • GrapheneOS — Privacy-hardened Android, runs on Google Pixel hardware. No Google services. Excellent security model. You can install sandboxed Google Play if needed for specific apps. For technically committed privacy users. Not for the faint of heart.
  • CalyxOS — De-Googled Android with microG (a free implementation of some Google services). More user-friendly than GrapheneOS for people who need some Android app compatibility.
  • Fairphone (Android + /e/ OS) — Ethically sourced, repairable hardware with de-Googled Android (/e/ OS) option. Available in Europe. Combines privacy and hardware ethics.

Migration steps

  1. If switching to iPhone, use the "Move to iOS" app to transfer contacts, photos, and messages
  2. Export Android contacts to a .vcf file before switching
  3. Identify which apps you use daily and confirm iOS equivalents exist
  4. Sign up for iCloud (5GB free; paid tiers for more)
  5. If staying on Android, install GrapheneOS (requires Pixel hardware; detailed instructions at grapheneos.org)
