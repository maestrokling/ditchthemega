Google Maps

Your location history is more revealing than you think

⚠️ The privacy case

In 2023, Google was ordered by the FTC to delete location data it had improperly retained. More significantly, Google has complied with "geofence warrants" — law enforcement requests for all devices located near a specific place at a specific time. Your location history on Google Maps is not just a convenience feature; it's a record of your life that third parties can access under certain legal circumstances.

What it is

Google Maps is the dominant navigation and mapping service with 1 billion monthly users. With location history enabled, Google records everywhere you go — every commute, every medical appointment, every place of worship, every late-night location. This data is stored indefinitely and has been used in criminal investigations.

What you lose

  • Google Maps' superior business data (reviews, hours, photos)
  • Live traffic and transit information
  • Integration with Google Search and Gmail
  • Street View

Honest assessment

Apple Maps and Google Maps are now genuinely competitive for navigation in most cities. The gap that existed 5 years ago is largely closed. For international travel, Google Maps still has better coverage in some regions. For US navigation, Apple Maps is excellent. The transition is lower-friction than most people expect.

Data to export first

  • Delete your Google Location History at myaccount.google.com/maps/yourdata
  • Turn off Location History in Google account settings
  • Export location data via Google Takeout if you want a record before deleting

Alternatives

  • Apple Maps — Privacy-first navigation. On-device processing where possible. No persistent location history sent to Apple. Excellent navigation quality in the US and Europe. Now includes indoor maps, transit, and cycling directions. Best choice for iPhone users.
  • OsmAnd Free; paid version available — Open-source, based on OpenStreetMap. Offline maps. No data collection. Works without internet. Best for privacy-focused users or frequent travelers who want offline capability.
  • Organic Maps — Free, open-source, based on OpenStreetMap. Beautiful interface. Offline-first. No tracking, no ads. Excellent for hiking and cycling. Simpler than OsmAnd.
  • HERE WeGo — Nokia-spinoff mapping service. Good offline maps. Less surveillance than Google. Good for car navigation.

Migration steps

  1. On iPhone, set Apple Maps as your default maps app (Settings → Maps)
  2. Use Apple Maps for 2 weeks for daily navigation before evaluating
  3. Delete your Google Location History at myaccount.google.com/maps/yourdata
  4. Turn off "Location History" in your Google Account settings
  5. Install OsmAnd or Organic Maps for offline maps when traveling internationally
