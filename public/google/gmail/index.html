Gmail

Leaving Google's email — and the inbox it's been reading

⚠️ The privacy case

Google's terms permit using the content of your emails for advertising targeting. While Google stopped using email content for personalized ads in Gmail in 2017, they still use data from Gmail (purchase confirmations, travel itineraries, subscriptions) to personalize ads across Google's network. Your inbox is also the recovery address for most of your other accounts — it's the master key to your digital life. Who holds that key matters.

What it is

Gmail is Google's free email service with 1.8 billion users. It offers 15GB free storage, excellent spam filtering, and deep integration with Google's other services. The tradeoff: Google scans email content to build advertising profiles. Every email you send or receive contributes to Google's picture of your interests, relationships, and behavior.

What you lose

  • 15GB free storage shared across Gmail, Drive, and Photos
  • Gmail's excellent spam filtering
  • Google integration (Calendar, Drive, Meet, etc.)
  • The familiarity of an interface you've used for years

Honest assessment

Email migration is real work. Your address is on everything. Changing it means updating accounts, notifying contacts, and dealing with email that keeps arriving at the old address for months. This is a multi-month project, not an afternoon task. The payoff is meaningful — email is the most sensitive data stream in most people's digital lives — but be realistic about the effort.

Data to export first

  • Use Google Takeout (takeout.google.com) to download all email as .mbox files
  • Export contacts (also via Takeout or contacts.google.com → Export)
  • Download Drive files if you use Google Drive
  • Note any Google services using this Gmail as the recovery/login email

Alternatives

  • Proton Mail Free up to 1GB; from $3.99/month for more storage — End-to-end encrypted. Based in Switzerland. Zero access to your email content. The most privacy-respecting major email provider. ProtonPass, ProtonVPN, and ProtonDrive available as a full ecosystem replacement.
  • Fastmail From $3/month — Fast, reliable, excellent interface. Not end-to-end encrypted by default, but no advertising and strong privacy practices. Good choice for users who want Gmail-quality UX without Google. Based in Australia.
  • Tutanota Free tier available; from €1/month — End-to-end encrypted. Open source. German-based. Strong privacy. Simpler than Proton.
  • Apple iCloud Mail Free with Apple ID (5GB shared) — Good option for Apple ecosystem users. Not end-to-end encrypted, but Apple has strong privacy practices and no ad business. Free with Apple devices.
  • Hey $99/year — Opinionated email redesign from Basecamp. No ads, no tracking. Different workflow (screening, stacks, imbox). Worth trying if you want to rethink email entirely.

Migration steps

  1. Export all Gmail via Google Takeout (takeout.google.com) — select Mail → export as .mbox
  2. Create new email account at Proton Mail (or your preferred alternative)
  3. Set up Gmail forwarding to your new address while transitioning (Gmail Settings → Forwarding)
  4. Update email on your most important accounts first — bank, financial, work, key services
  5. Add a Gmail auto-reply noting your new email address
  6. Over the next 30–60 days, update remaining accounts as you encounter them
  7. After 3–6 months, when you stop seeing important mail arrive at Gmail, close the account
