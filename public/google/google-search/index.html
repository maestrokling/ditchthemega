Google Search

The hardest habit to break — and the most worth breaking

⚠️ The privacy case

Google's search history is among the most intimate data it holds. People search things they wouldn't tell their doctor, their spouse, or their employer. Google keeps this history indefinitely unless you manually delete it. In 2023, Google was ordered to pay $5 billion in a class action lawsuit over tracking users in "Incognito" mode, which turned out not to be as private as users believed.

What it is

Google Search processes 8.5 billion searches per day and holds roughly 92% of the global search market. Every search you make contributes to a detailed profile of your interests, concerns, health questions, political views, and intentions. Google uses this data for advertising across its entire network — not just on Google Search, but on every site running Google Ads.

What you lose

  • Google's search quality, which is genuinely better for some query types
  • Google Maps integration in search results
  • Google Shopping integration
  • Personalized results based on your history and location

Honest assessment

DuckDuckGo and Brave Search have closed the quality gap significantly in the last few years. For most queries, they return comparable results. Google remains better for local searches, very recent news, and highly specific technical queries. Many people who switch report that they barely notice the difference after the first week.

Alternatives

  • DuckDuckGo — No tracking. No search history. No profiling. Results are good for most queries. !Bangs feature lets you search other sites directly (e.g. !a for Amazon, !w for Wikipedia). The default privacy-respecting search choice.
  • Brave Search — Independent search index (not Google or Bing results). Completely private. Strong results. Part of the Brave browser ecosystem but works in any browser.
  • Startpage — Returns Google results without the tracking. Privacy proxy for Google. Good transition option if you want Google quality without Google tracking.
  • Kagi From $5/month — Paid search — the theory being that if you're not the product, you're the customer. Highly customizable. Excellent results. No ads. Growing following among technical users.
  • Ecosia — Uses ad revenue to plant trees. Privacy better than Google. Results powered by Bing.

Migration steps

  1. Change your browser's default search engine to DuckDuckGo or Brave Search
  2. {'Chrome': 'Settings → Search engine → Change to DuckDuckGo'}
  3. {'Safari': 'Settings → Safari → Search Engine → DuckDuckGo'}
  4. {'Firefox': 'Settings → Search → Default Search Engine → DuckDuckGo'}
  5. Use it for 2 weeks before evaluating — the habit takes time to break
  6. If you find DuckDuckGo results lacking for specific queries, add "!g" to search Google directly (DuckDuckGo bang syntax) rather than switching back entirely
  7. Delete your Google Search history at myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy → Delete activity
